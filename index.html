Principal's Message | Mensaje del Director

Dear colleagues,

As we approach this week before Thanksgiving, I want to reflect on an essential truth about education: knowledge is not compartmentalized into isolated subjects, but rather a rich tapestry where disciplines interweave and strengthen one another.

In the real world, solving complex problems requires drawing from mathematics, science, language, history, and the arts simultaneously. A sustainable solution to environmental challenges needs scientific understanding, mathematical modeling, historical context, persuasive communication, and creative thinkingall working together as one.

This is why our shift toward multidisciplinary projects is so important. We are not simply changing how we assess; we are changing how we teach our students to think. When they learn to see connections across disciplines, they develop the kind of integrated thinking that will serve them throughout their lives.

As we begin implementing these projects, I encourage you to embrace the collaboration required. Talk with your colleagues across departments. Find the natural intersections between your subjects. Model for students what it means to think interdisciplinarily.

This approach is challenging, but it is also deeply meaningful work. Thank you for your commitment to this vision.
